🏛️ About Itchan Kala

Itchan Kala is the inner fortress of Khiva, a living museum of Islamic architecture and Central Asian history.

Itchan Kala (meaning "Inner City" in Uzbek) is the historic core of Khiva. Surrounded by massive clay walls that have stood for centuries, this UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the best-preserved examples of a Silk Road city. Inside its 2.5-kilometer walls, you'll find over 50 historic monuments, including madrassahs, mosques, caravanserais, and palaces — many dating back to the 14th century.

The city was a major stop along the ancient Silk Road, where traders from China, Persia, India, and Europe would rest, trade, and pray. Its architecture reflects a blend of Persian, Turkic, and Islamic influences, with intricate turquoise tiles, carved wooden columns, and majestic domes.

✨ Landmarks Inside Itchan Kala

🕌

Kalta Minor Minaret

A striking, turquoise-tiled minaret that remains unfinished but stands as one of the most iconic symbols of the city.

🌄

Islam Khodja Complex

Features the city's tallest minaret, offering panoramic views of the entire oasis for those who climb its narrow steps.

🕊️

Juma Mosque

A unique 10th-century mosque known for its large prayer hall supported by over 200 beautifully carved wooden pillars.

🏰

Tash Hauli Palace

Known as the "Stone Palace," it is decorated with intricate stucco, frescoes, and spectacular carved ceilings.

📍 Top Landmarks Inside Itchan Kala

🕌

Kalta Minor Minaret

An unfinished turquoise-tiled minaret that was meant to be the tallest in Central Asia. It remains Khiva's most iconic symbol.

🏛️

Kunya Ark

The palace fortress of Khiva's rulers, featuring a watchtower, throne room, harem, and the ceremonial Kurnysh-Dar square.

🕊️

Juma Mosque

A 10th-century mosque with a large prayer hall supported by over 200 beautifully carved wooden pillars.

🌄

Islam Khodja Complex

Features the city's tallest minaret, offering panoramic views of the entire oasis for those who climb its narrow steps.

🏰

Tash Hauli Palace

Known as the "Stone Palace," it is decorated with intricate stucco, frescoes, and spectacular carved ceilings.

🌙

Pahlavan Mahmud Mausoleum

Dedicated to the patron saint and poet of Khiva, this peaceful mausoleum is a place of reverence and craftsmanship.

📜 History Timeline

6th Century

Khiva established as a small settlement along the Silk Road.

10th Century

Juma Mosque built — the oldest standing structure in Itchan Kala.

14th–15th Century

Golden age of Khiva — major monuments built, city becomes a key Silk Road hub.

18th–19th Century

Kalta Minor and Tash Hauli Palace constructed during Khiva's last flourishing period.

1990

Itchan Kala inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2024

Restoration efforts continue to preserve this timeless treasure for future generations.

🧳 Visitor Information

⏰

Opening Hours

Itchan Kala is open for every one 24/7. However, the specific monuments, museums, and ticketed attractions inside the complex do have set operating hours.

🎟️

Ticket Price

Entry to Itchan Kala is completely free. Only some monuments, madrassas and museums may require entry tickets.

👗

Dress Code

Modest clothing is recommended when visiting mosques and mausoleums. Shoulders and knees should be covered due to religious customs.

📸

Photography

Photography is allowed in most areas, but some museums may charge a small fee for cameras.
