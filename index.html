Why Khiva is unforgettable

Khiva is one of the best-preserved historic cities in Central Asia. Its old town, Itchan Kala, is a UNESCO World Heritage site, surrounded by red clay walls and filled with palace courtyards, grand mosques, and atmospheric bazaars.

The historic inner city containing more than 50 historic monuments and hundreds of traditional dwellings.

Kalta Minor Minaret

A striking, turquoise-tiled minaret that remains unfinished but stands as one of the most iconic symbols of the city.

Islam Khodja Complex

Features the city's tallest minaret, offering panoramic views of the entire oasis for those who climb its narrow steps.

Juma Mosque

A unique 10th-century mosque known for its large prayer hall supported by over 200 beautifully carved wooden pillars.

Hidden stories and cultural charm

Every corner of Khiva carries a story. Caravanserais once welcomed traders from China, Persia, and Europe. Madrasahs taught poetry, astronomy, and religion. The city retains the rhythm of daily life, with craftsmen, cafes, and musicians keeping tradition alive.

📍 Around Khiva

🏜️

Desert Safari

Experience the vast Kyzylkum Desert with camel rides and stunning sunset views just outside Khiva.

📍 Kyzylkum Desert, 30km from Khiva
🏰

Ancient Fortresses

Visit the ruins of Toprak-Kala and Ayaz-Kala, ancient fortresses that once guarded the Silk Road.

📍 Toprak-Kala, 60km from Khiva
🏛️

Nuratau Mountains

Discover the scenic mountain range with ancient petroglyphs, hiking trails, and traditional villages.

📍 Nuratau Mountains, 200km from Khiva
🧵

Silk Carpet Workshop

Visit a traditional carpet workshop in the village of Bogot, where silk carpets are woven by hand.

📍 Bogot Village, 20km from Khiva

🗺️ Find Khiva on the Map

Tash Hauli Palace

Known as the "Stone Palace," Tash Hauli is decorated with intricate stucco, frescoes, and spectacular carved ceilings.

Mausoleum of Pahlavan Mahmud

Dedicated to the patron saint and poet of Khiva, this peaceful mausoleum is a place of reverence and craftsmanship.

Silk Road Heritage

Khiva was a key trading hub. Its market streets still echo with the exchange of carpets, ceramics, spices, and stories.

Local Cuisine

Sample plov, manti, shurpa, and sweet halva at small family-run restaurants in the old city.

📍 Find Itchan Kala

The West Gate (Ota Darvoza) is the main entrance to the historic inner city.

Quick tips for visitors

  • Best time to visit: spring and autumn, when the weather is mild.
  • Carry cash for small shops and tea houses inside Itchan Kala.
  • Climb the minarets for sweeping views of the ancient city.
  • Leave time to explore at dusk, when the lights highlight the carved facades.
